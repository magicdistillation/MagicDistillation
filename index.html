MagicDistillation

Weak-to-Strong Distribution Matching Distillation for Efficient Large-Scale Portrait Video Synthesis

Anonymous Authors

Recently, open-source video diffusion models (VDMs), such as WanX and HunyuanVideo, have been scaled to over 10 billion (10B+) parameters. Fine-tuning these large-scale VDMs for portrait video synthesis has shown significant improvements across several dimensions, including enhanced visual quality and more natural facial motion dynamics. However, despite these advancements, large-scale VDMs currently require approximately 10 minutes to synthesize a 28-step video on a single H100 GPU, which falls far short of real-time performance. Therefore, reducing the computational overhead of these models is a critical challenge. To address this issue, we propose MagicDistillation, a novel framework that mitigates both the memory constraints during training and the challenges of training collapse commonly observed in vanilla DMD. Specifically , we first utilize LoRA to fine-tune the fake DiT, effectively resolving the out-of-memory problem. Next, we apply Weak-to-Strong (W2S) distribution matching, which addresses the mismatch between the video synthesized by the few-step generator and the real data distribution, a key factor leading to inaccuracies in KL divergence approximation. Furthermore, we minimize the distance between the fake data distribution and the ground truth distribution to enhance the visual fidelity of the synthesized videos. Experimental results on HunyuanVideo demonstrate the effectiveness of MagicDistillation, surpassing standard Euler, LCM, and DMD baselines in both FID/FVD metrics and VBench for 1/4-step portrait video synthesis.
